Alexandros Tavernarakis

Physicist - Researcher

I am a Belgian-born and Greek-raised physicist with academic and professional experience in France, Spain and Greece.

I studied physics in the University of Crete and continued with post-graduate studies (masterâ€™s and doctroral degree) in France (Grenoble, Lyon, Paris) where I speccialized in experimental quantum optics. Upon completion of my PhD, I took up a Research Fellow position in the Institute of Photonic Sciences (ICFO), Barcelona.

My research activities are within the field of experimental optomechanics. I'm aiming at developing novel, ultra-sensitive techniques for measuring tiny resonators. My professional goal is two-fold: a) exploring the fundamental limits of the optomechanical coupling and b) developing sensing applications based on continuously measuring the motion of a nano-resonator.

After a fulfilling journey in academia, nostalgia motivated me to return in Greece and bring my expertise into the industry and a couple of small and dynamic high-tech companies in Greece.

I grew up enjoying free diving in the Mediterannean, playing tennis and having deep reverence for cinema, hard-rock music and books. Living in Catalunya, I discovered the joy of rock climbing and mountain hiking.
